RESEARCH ASSISTANT

Western University Research Assistant

At Western University, through a Co-Operative Education Program (Co-Op), I was able to assist Masters and Ph.D. Students with their research. Each university lab was supervised by a faculty member, and I worked day-to-day, side-by-side with graduate students working on their research for their Masters, Ph.D. and Post-doctoral fellow degrees.

In my Research Assistance position in Mobile Genetics at Dr. Dave Edgell's lab, under the guidance of lab manager Nancy Friedrich, we were looking to genetically disrupt the growth of a water-bourne pathogen by using recombinant (specifically-modified) DNA.


A Petri dish with Aeromonas hydrophila culture streaked in blood-red agar

Laboratory Bench

Writing Reports

Writing notes and reports, in the adjunct study room.

Pipetting

In the lab using a precisely-tuned device ("Pipette") to transfer a specific quantity of liquid to a curvette. The Laboratory Technician was taking my picture and I knew it, so I had a smile.

Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR)

A classic Biometra Polmerase Chain Reaction (PCR) Thermal Cycler. PCR amplifies a sample, so you start with some sample, and you get a lot of sample after it's finished its cycles.

PCR Receipe

Depending on the material being amplified, it is important to know your recipe. Also since the lab book is a permanent record, the next person looking at my work will know what I did, and how they can build upon my work.

Very Cultured

Classic Petri dish with 24 colonies under research (mobE N27A sequence in Aeromonas hydrophila). This was later analysized using a blot.

Southern Blot

This is a process of gel electrophoresis to separate and purify the sample into a ladder-like effect. It works well and I always set this before lunch.

Ladder Image

The ladder can be seen on the imaging computer, and shows where my virus sequence is expected in number of pairs.

End Game

At the end of the process, you want a way to quantify what you actually have. This is a picture of the actual genetic sequence I introduced into the virus within the organism. Note this is many pages long, and it's just the target location of interest, not the full genetic sequence.

End Game on an iMac

This is the same sequence but on an iMac computer, using DNASTAR's Lasergene Software.
