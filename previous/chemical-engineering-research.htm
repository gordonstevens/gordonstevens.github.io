RESEARCH ASSISTANT

Western University Research Assistant

At Western University, through a Co-Operative Education Program (Co-Op), I was able to assist a Ph.D. Candidate with his research. Each university lab was supervised by a faculty member, and I worked day-to-day, side-by-side with him to learn about Chemical Engineering Research.

In my Research Assistance position in Chemical Engineering in Prof. Jesse Zhu's lab, under the guidance of Ph.D. Candidate, Dongbing Li, we were quantifying all aspects of a novel circulating fluidized bed chemical reactor design.

 

Reactor sensor-port adjustment

Gordon Stevens adjusting a port which a sensor connects to, to measure flow velocity in that section of the circulating fluidized bed chemical reactor. Under the brilliant mentor and visionary, Dongbing Li.

Adjusting components

The then-Ph.D. Candidate Dongbing Li adjusting positions of the chemical reactor components, now a leading international business entrepreneur and successful post-doctoral fellow.
It is often said it is the people who make the difference, which couldn't be more true, Dongbing Li is a true hero and mentor.

Prototype shielding tub

Signal degredation was experienced for all inputs, and environmental factors were suspected. Gordon Stevens shielded sensitive electronic connections using a prototype plastic tub. This quick prototype saved valuable input data from sensors throughout the system.

Lower-level transparent-view window

We run titanium dioxide through the reactor, as it is an excellent average-sized analyte. Especially after I suggested we normalize the particulate by sieveing, and using a kitchen blender to normalize the particulate. Sometimes the easiest and least expensive method is the best. "This is the way."

Side-view of the operator station

From this side-view, you can see the various valves and level indicators. This is the main control station for the reactor. In the rear-right is our equipment workbench, and the middle-right is the data collection system.

Ground-level rear-view

Note the cabling used to transport signals from sensors to the data collection tub, as well as transpaent air hoses. The ports along the entirety of the reactor are connected to glass bottles to collect any loose particles, which would cause data errors.

View of analytical ports

These ports are perpendicular to the flow, and are built into the reactor at salient points along the length of the reactor. Note the unused, capped port versus the in-use port with transparent hose.

That is quick-sand

The sand (titanium dioxide) is a solid behaving like a gas moving through reactor with high-velocity. The bottle-trap is quite noticeable from the column, to the bottle-trap, to the flow sensor just out of picture, which turns the measurement into an electronic signal, read at the operator system.

Bottles and pipe

Another picture of the pipework, viewport pipe section, bottle traps, and circuit board, which determines various quantities of the analogue input, and sends a digital signal down the wire which represents the input, is collected by one board, and is then interpreted by the software on the operator computer.

Data collection and LabView

Using National Instruments LabView, Microsoft Excel, MatLab, and some other software, the signal data is transformed into useful information about the operations of the chemical reactor. Once the data is collected in a constructive manner, the data is transformed in many ways, for different purposes and reports.

More of a feelings picture

This picture is just for scale, perspective, and feelings of how I can advance technology for the benefit of mankind in the future.

When this picture was taken, the form and function of the reactor came into full-view.

High-grade components

Seemingly, sometimes you cannot have enough components ready to go. Of course these are research-grade so they cost twice as much.

Results-oriented

"Results-oriented" is an often-used catch-phrase, but in this case, the results were erratic, so I made short work of getting it to the repair bench, cleaning it out, checking internal components, and getting it back to operation in less than 10 minutes.

Double-check the double-check

While we check the velocity using bottle-trapped circuit-board mounted components and sending the signal to the operator computer for further analysis, it is important to trust and double-check these readings using another method. These boxes measure the velocity of the particulate.

Transparent-view for the operator

From the operator seat, this picture shows the view of the fast-moving particulate, with a long-probe sensor hooked-up to the velocity measurers. In another port out-of-picture, the circuit-board sensor measures the same velocity for comparison. The computer is just a normal desktop computer, attached to the external data collection board.

The Next Phase

After my inspirational work with Dongbing Li, I was sure that Chemical Engineering was an exciting and rewarding pursuit, I would continue to Loyalist College Chemical Engineering Technology. During my work at Loyalist College, I specialised in extraction technology, using an extraction method called supercritical fluid extraction creating a liquid extracted from plant material. Over the coursework, my partner and I successfully extracted nepetalactone, the main organic compound found in the catnip plant, and a liquid material was produced. Other interesting active compounds and products can be extracted from: mushrooms, cinnamon, cannabis, chili peppers, black pepper, and more.
